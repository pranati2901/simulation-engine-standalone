EV Charging — Scenario & Resilience Engine

Data we would need from your side to model your network

NextXR · SimCore
Purpose. To simulate faults and cascades across your charging network — and quantify the revenue, energy and SLA impact — the engine builds a live model of your real assets. Below is the minimal data set required. Almost all of it is standard OCPP / CMS export; nothing bespoke. A pilot can start with as few as 3–5 stations and expand from there.

1 · Asset inventory from your CMS / CPMS

DataExample fieldsWhy the engine needs it
Stationsid, name, location, site typeEach station becomes a node in the model
Chargersid, OCPP id, AC/DC, power (kW), connector, statusDefines what can fail and its capacity

2 · Electrical topology site design / single-line diagram

DataExample fieldsWhy the engine needs it
Connectivitycharger → feeder → transformer → substationDefines the cascade — what goes down when an upstream asset trips
Ratingstransformer kVA, feeder capacity kW, BESS kWh/kWSets overload / thermal thresholds

3 · Operational telemetry OCPP + meters

DataExample fieldsWhy the engine needs it
SessionsStatusNotification, MeterValues (kWh), start/stopUtilisation & revenue base per station
Load & thermalfeeder load kW, transformer / BESS temp °CTriggers for overload and thermal runaway

4 · Fault history monitoring / CMMS

DataExample fieldsWhy the engine needs it
Past incidentsasset, fault type, count (12 mo), mean repair hoursSets real failure probabilities & repair times — not guesses

5 · Commercial data utility & CPO contracts

DataExample fieldsWhy the engine needs it
Tariffstime-of-use energy price, demand charge (per kW)Real cost of curtailment & demand spikes
Revenueprice to driver per kWh / per sessionRevenue lost when a station is down
SLA termsuptime target, penalty per hour down, reimbursementsPenalty exposure when uptime is breached

6 · Demand forecast optional — you already produce this

Your existing demand forecast feeds the engine directly as the stress-test input (e.g. "next summer peak"), so the simulation runs against your own projections.

Format, scope & security

  • Format: CSV or JSON export, or a read-only API. We are hardware-agnostic and speak standard OCPP — no changes to your stack.
  • Minimum to start: items 1, 2 and 5 for 3–5 stations. Items 3 and 4 sharpen accuracy but the model runs without a full history.
  • Security: read-only access; data can be anonymised (station codes, not addresses). No control commands are ever issued to your chargers.
